# GHG Emissions and Climate Change Risk Assessment

## Project Overview
This report presents a greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions assessment and climate change risk 
analysis for Akçansa Çimento Sanayi A.Ş., one of Turkey's leading cement producers, 
using IPCC-compliant methodologies.

## Case Study
**Company:** Akçansa Çimento Sanayi A.Ş. (Heidelberg Materials & Sabancı Holding)
**Sector:** Cement Industry
**Scope:** Scope 1, Scope 2, Scope 3 emissions (2019-2024)

## Methodology
- **IPCC GHG Inventory Guidelines** (1996, 2006) for emission calculations
- **GHG Protocol Framework** for Scope 1, 2, 3 classification
- **TCFD Framework** for climate risk assessment
- Emission formula: ECO₂ = Clinker Production (t) × 0.507 × (1 + CKD%)

## Key Findings
- Akçansa's carbon intensity (620 kg CO₂/t cement) is below the national average (670 kg CO₂/t)
- Scope 1 emissions declined from 2022 to 2023 due to alternative fuel use and clinker substitution
- Scope 3 emissions show an upward trend, highlighting growing value chain impacts
- Climate risks intensify significantly beyond +2 warming scenarios

## Climate Risk Assessment
Physical and transition risks assessed across four warming scenarios including:
- Extreme heat and water scarcity risks
- EU ETS and CBAM transition risks
- Alignment with Turkey's 2053 Net Zero target

## Technologies & Frameworks Used
- IPCC AR6 Climate Scenarios (SSP1-1.9 to SSP5-8.5)
- GHG Protocol
- TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures)
- ESG Reporting Standards
